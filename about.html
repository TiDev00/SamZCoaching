Who am I?

Biography

For the past 2 years, El Hadj has been responsible for import compliance at Natural Resources Canada. He is a specialist in governance and capacity building for the Canadian public service, contributing to poverty reduction, promoting good governance, and international trade by enhancing the knowledge and skills of developing countries. He assists in establishing sustainable institutions, structures, and processes. He has solid experience in project management in developing countries, specifically in project design aligned with strategic objectives, project submission for funding, evaluation, recommendations, and lessons learned. He has worked for approximately 11 years in international development. Before that, he worked as a financial advisor and credit analyst in several major Canadian financial institutions, including the Royal Bank of Canada for 7 years. He has substantial experience in managing multidimensional projects and has collaborated with various international organizations (World Bank, United Nations, World Customs Organization, etc.). His experience also includes field operations management in Latin America (Colombia), Asia (Afghanistan, Pakistan), and the Caribbean (Haiti). He possesses diversified experience in the challenges and constraints faced by developing countries and fragile states and the means to overcome them. El Hadj holds two master's degrees, one in organizational governance for international development (international economics) from the University Pierre Mendes France (Grenoble, France), and a Master of Business Administration from the Institute of Business Administration (Lille-France). He also holds a certificate in financial planning from the University of Quebec in Canada.
